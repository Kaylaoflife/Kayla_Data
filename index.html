Bank Loan Analysis

In this project, I analyzed 23 years of loan data issued across various cities and age groups. The goal was to understand who gets approved for loans and identify any patterns or bias in the process. Younger adults (18–24) and seniors (65–100) had the highest approval rates, which raised some fairness questions. Middle-aged customers, especially those between 45 and 54, had the highest number of complaints despite lower approval rates. Though women had a slightly higher approval rate on average, men had more approved loans in most years. Auto loans recorded the highest approval rates, but mortgage loans showed steadier trends over the years. Cities like San Antonio, Mesa, Detroit, and San Jose stood out with unusual approval trends. About 33% of all feedback was complaint-related, with middle-aged users submitting the most concerns. These findings point to the need for fairer scoring systems and clearer communication with applicants. This report helps decision-makers spot gaps and improve loan strategies going forward.
